Crispy Fried Chicken

Description

Fried chicken is a popular dish eaten all over the world consisting of chicken covered in batter and breading. It is deep-fried allowing it to be crispy on the outside and juicy and tender on the inside.

Ingredients

  • 1 (4 pound) chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour for coating
  • salt and pepper
  • 2 quarts vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. Take your cut up chicken pieces and skin them if you prefer
  2. Place flour in a large plastic bag depending on the amount of chicken you are cooking. Season flour with paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Dip chicken pieces in buttermilk then, a few at a time, transfer them into the bag with flour; seal the bag and shake to coat well.
  4. Place the coated chicken on a cookie sheet or tray, and cover with a clean dish towel or waxed paper. Let it sit until the flour is of a paste-like consistency
  5. Fill a large skillet about 1/3 to 1/2 full with vegetable oil. Heat until very hot.
  6. Put in as many chicken pieces as the skillet can hold. Brown the chicken in hot oil on both sides.
  7. When browned, reduce heat and cover skillet; let cook for 30 minutes. Remove cover, raise heat again, and continue to fry until crispy.
  8. Drain fried chicken on paper towels. Depending on how much chicken you have, you may have to fry in a few shifts. Keep the finished chicken in a slightly warm oven while preparing the rest.
